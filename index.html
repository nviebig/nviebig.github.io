About

I'm a physics master's student at ETH Zürich. My programme spans theoretical and computational physics, and I've spent most of my time on problems where simulation and data meet.

Right now I'm doing my master's thesis at the University of Oxford. The project is about making a spectral weather model differentiable so that gradients can flow through the simulation and be used to learn better model parameters.

More broadly, I work on climate models, machine learning, and scientific computing. I like building things — models, simulations, and tools that help me (or others) understand physical systems.
